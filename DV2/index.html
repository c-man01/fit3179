Bicycle Hire in London

Where & When?

Cycling in London is a popular mode of transport and leisure activity within the capital city of the United Kingdom. The 2010 launch of the Santander Cycle Hire system by Transport for London (TfL) has contributed to considerable growth in the uptake of cycling in London. The TfL’s bicycle hire data is made available to the general public and here we will present some interesting features of this data for the year 2019.

As seen in the map of London to the right, London’s bicycle hire system is somewhat geographically limited with only 12 of its 34 boroughs having hire stations present. Unsurprisingly, with its minute residential population and high commuter traffic, the City of London has the greatest amount of bicycle hires per capita of all of London’s boroughs.

Producing around ¼ of UK GDP in 2019, London hosts a bustling economy and business district. Because of this it comes as no surprise seeing Londoners hiring tendencies mimic the 9-5 working week.

The Yearly Cycle

Even with its infamously poor weather, you’ll still see in excess of 500,000 people saddling up any given month! We do however see significant seasonal fluctuations in cycle hire with the Winter months being as much as 50% less popular that its Summer peak counter-part; for July we saw a peak of 1,097,803 hires.

Cheques & Balances

In 2019 there were 763 active bicycle stations, each with varying levels of popularity; some stations has just over 1200 interactions (bicycle hires or drop-offs) whereas the most popular station Belgrove St, Kings Cross had nearly 170,000 interactions in 2019.

Also seen in this visualisation is a description of where are the most popular departure and arrival points for bicycle hires. As a result of this were also able to see which stations might be experiencing an under or overflow of bicycle drop-offs. With the largest bicycle surplus being 16,930 and the most significant deficit being -12,291, the flow of bicycles would be of great interest to the TfL so measures can be taken to ensure all stations are sufficiently stocked with bikes.
